To Improve, a Candidate at this Level Should:
  • Practice actively listening to spoken language delivered at fast, native speed from a variety of sources, e.g., live or broadcast television and/or radio.
  • Practice giving clear, smoothly flowing, elaborate and vivid descriptions, using appropriate word stress and intonation to guide listeners to notice and remember significant points.
  • Practice monitoring your speech for grammatical consistency and smoothness, quickly substituting an equivalent term for a word you can't recall.
  • Practice reading lengthy, complex texts (journals, novels, biographies, etc.) within and outside of your interests and/or field.
  • Make an effort to expand vocabulary by actively learning synonyms or similar expressions to what you already know.
  • Practice writing arguments, explaining significant points, presenting supporting details, and rounding off with an appropriate conclusion.
